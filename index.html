Tiling Problem

Summary This assignment presents students with a variant of an interactive tiling problem involving multiple constraints. Students are tasked with exploring search trees and traversing search graphs to find solutions using any AI search algorithm of their choice. Additionally, students are encouraged to develop custom heuristics to increase complexity by adversarially tiling the plane.
Topics Search Problems, Constraint Satisfaction, Heuristics
Audience Suitable for undergraduate or graduate students enrolled in introductory courses on Artificial Intelligence or Machine Learning.
Difficulty Moderate difficulty for undergraduates with a background in Python programming. Previous implementations have shown that most students successfully completed the assignment within the allotted time.
Strengths This assignment encourages students to design and implement their own search techniques. It supports the application of various algorithms, including BFS, DFS, A*, Genetic Algorithms, and Reinforcement Learning, providing flexibility in problem-solving approaches.
Weaknesses The primary challenge is that students are expected to have a strong proficiency in Python, which may pose difficulties for those with less experience in the language.
Dependencies A solid background in Python programming is recommended. The assignment is compatible with Python 3+ and can be executed on most single-board computers, ensuring accessibility across various platforms.
Variants This assignment is highly adaptable. Instructors can adjust the focus to specific algorithms or modify the complexity by altering the number and nature of constraints. These modifications allow the assignment to be customized for different levels of difficulty and learning objectives.
